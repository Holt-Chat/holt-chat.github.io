The signature

Status that's actually true.

Presence isn't a green dot you set once and forget. Holt reflects what's really happening, so you know whether a message lands now or waits for morning. Here's what each one looks like to the people you talk to.

Typing indicators travel the same way, peer to peer and ephemeral. The ripple shows up, then it's gone. Nothing about it is ever stored.

The encryption manifest

What's sealed, and what's shared.

Encryption happens on your device before anything leaves it. Here's exactly what the server can never read, and the few things you choose to share so chat still feels live.

  • Encrypted
    Message contents & files Sealed with a per-channel AES key, wrapped to each member's RSA public key. Rotated on join and leave for forward secrecy. The server stores ciphertext it can't read.
  • Encrypted
    Your private key Generated in your browser and saved to a .keys file you keep. It never leaves your device, so no one signs in as you without it.
  • Visible
    Presence & typing Online, idle, do-not-disturb and when you're typing are shared so chat feels live. Set yourself to invisible and none of it goes out. Status is yours to flip, anytime.

Read the full protocol in the docs. It's open, so you don't have to take our word for it.

One-to-one messages, group chats, and broadcast channels, with roles, permissions, and invites built in.

Voice and video calls

Calls flow directly between people over WebRTC. The server routes the handshake and never sees the stream.

Record a voice note or drop a file. Media is encrypted the same way your messages are, handled in one interface.
